Our Honest Take
Who each tool is actually built for
We'd rather give you a straight answer than a sales pitch.
Joist
Good starter app. Limited ceiling.
Joist is easy to get started with and the free plan is solid for basic estimates and invoices. If all you need is a simple invoice on your phone, Joist works. Once you need eSign, change orders, client notifications, or anything beyond two doc types, you've hit its ceiling.
Invoice2go
Invoice-first. Built for simplicity.
Invoice2go is clean and mobile-friendly, and the lower pricing makes it appealing. But it's focused almost entirely on invoicing — no eSign, no AI, no job tracking. If invoices are your only need and you're price-sensitive, it's a reasonable pick.
Jobber
Enterprise-grade. Priced to match.
Jobber is the most full-featured option — scheduling, dispatch, GPS tracking, team management. It's built for operations running crews. At $29–$249+/mo it's priced that way too. If you're a solo contractor or small crew focused on documents, proposals, and getting paid, you're paying for a lot you won't use.
BuildOrder
Best for: solo contractors and small crews who need professional docs fast.
If your priority is generating a sharp estimate or contract in two minutes, sending it with one click, and collecting a deposit before you leave the driveway — BuildOrder is purpose-built for that. The AI, native eSign, and payment-in-one-link combination isn't available elsewhere at this price. We don't have a native mobile app or full scheduling yet — that's honest.
Common Questions
Frequently Asked
Can I switch to BuildOrder from Joist without losing my data?
Yes. You can start a BuildOrder account for free and run both tools side by side while you migrate. There's no lock-in. You export your Joist data and re-enter what you need in BuildOrder — most contractors complete this in under an hour.
Does BuildOrder have a mobile app?
Not yet. BuildOrder is a mobile-optimized web app — it works well on any phone browser, but it's not listed on the App Store or Google Play. A native app is on the roadmap. If a native app is a hard requirement today, Joist or Jobber have that.
What makes the eSign + payment combination different?
With BuildOrder, your client receives one link. They read the contract, sign it, and pay the deposit — all in one browser session without creating an account or downloading anything. With Jobber, eSign requires a separate DocuSign integration. Joist and Invoice2go don't offer eSign at all.
Is BuildOrder a Joist alternative?
For most residential contractors, yes. If you currently use Joist for estimates and invoices but wish you had AI generation, eSign, change orders, lien waivers, or a client portal — BuildOrder covers all of that. The free plan is comparable to Joist's free tier.
Is BuildOrder a Jobber alternative?
For document-heavy workflows, yes. BuildOrder is not a replacement for Jobber's scheduling, dispatch, or GPS features. If your crew needs route optimization or field service management, Jobber is built for that. If your priority is generating professional contracts and estimates quickly, BuildOrder does it better and at a fraction of the cost.
